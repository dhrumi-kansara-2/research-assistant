**Draft Report: Impact of AI on Healthcare (≤200 words)**  

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is reshaping healthcare through both transformative benefits and emerging challenges.  

**Positive Impacts**  
- **Enhanced Diagnostics**: Machine‑learning models analyze imaging and pathology data with accuracy rivaling or surpassing clinicians, enabling earlier disease detection.  
- **Personalized Medicine**: AI integrates genomic, clinical, and lifestyle data to tailor treatment plans, improving outcomes and reducing trial‑and‑error prescribing.  
- **Operational Efficiency**: Predictive analytics streamline resource allocation, reduce appointment wait times, and lower administrative costs.  
- **Drug Discovery**: AI accelerates target identification and virtual screening, cutting development timelines and costs.  

**Negative Impacts**  
- **Bias and Inequity**: Training data gaps can perpetuate disparities, leading to unequal care quality across populations.  
- **Privacy and Security**: Large‑scale data use heightens risks of breaches and misuse of sensitive health information.  
- **Clinical Reliability**: Overreliance on AI can erode clinician judgment; algorithmic errors may cause misdiagnoses.  
- **Workforce Disruption**: Automation threatens certain roles, necessitating reskilling and new regulatory frameworks.  

**Conclusion**  
AI’s dual nature demands balanced governance: robust validation, transparency, and ethical oversight to maximize benefits while mitigating risks.
